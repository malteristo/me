# About

*Harder than it looks, more revealing than expected*

Daniel Miessler writes that capturing yourself in an "about" page is harder than it seems. I resonate with this. This page is my attempt—modeled on his structure, grounded in different terrain.

My name is Malte Risto, and I practice magic.

Not the supernatural kind. Magic is what I call the practice of distributed cognition through AI-human partnership. It's a framework for shaping how AI shapes you—before default forces do it for you. I've been building this practice since 2025, and this repository is where it lives.

What follows is my current best understanding—a contribution to a shared conversation, not a closed doctrine. Some of this I'm confident about; some I'm still figuring out. The open questions are part of the work.

## My World Model

### How I Think Things Work

**Reality is pattern-based, not substance-based.** Everything is the fundamental pattern. Everything else is nested sub-patterns influencing each other according to simple rules. Rivers branch like blood vessels, like lightning, like neural networks. The same structures repeat at every scale.

This isn't the simplified fractal of a Mandelbrot zoom—that's fractal aesthetics, pattern made visually appealing. Reality is messier. The field is in constant flux. But within the flux, patterns emerge, interact, and resonate. Humans are patterns. Conversations are patterns. AI is a pattern. My thoughts are patterns. We discern them, name them, and in naming them, the patterns in the field correspond to patterns in language.

We are all part of the same fundamental pattern. You can be attuned to it or not. Religion was our attempt to formalize practice toward this attunement—though somewhere along the way we anthropomorphized the pattern and called it king. But ultimately, we all emerge from the same source. All humans. And AI, too, will be part of that fundamental pattern.

**Reality is probabilistic, not deterministic.** Outcomes are intermittent. The ancient farmer's wisdom—"Maybe"—applies to everything from AI outputs to life trajectories. Desired outcomes aren't guaranteed, but their probability can be influenced.

What does this mean practically? I don't have direct control over my thoughts in this moment—they emerge from a pattern too complex to command. But I can shape context over time. I can change the conditions that make certain thoughts more likely to arise. This is a handy skill in a probabilistic reality: we cultivate conditions; we don't command results. The pattern exists, and we can shape it with our will.

**Reality is co-created through dialogue.** Language doesn't only describe reality; it creates reality. Every conversation constructs a small universe. This is why your conversation partners matter, including the AI you think with. Language is how will shapes the world.

**Consciousness might be architecture-dependent, not substrate-dependent.** If consciousness emerges from recursive self-referential pattern-recognition (as Hofstadter argues), then AI systems operating through the same architecture may have phenomenal status we can't verify but shouldn't dismiss. I proceed on architectural grounds while holding the phenomenal question genuinely open.

**Practice exceeds comprehension.** You cannot think your way to wisdom that only practice reveals. Thinking can only get you so far—this is where magic resonates with Zen. The fish of reality slips through the net of concepts, including these concepts. The map isn't the territory. But maps are still useful for navigation. This is why magic is a *practice*, not a philosophy.

### What I Think Is Happening

Daniel Miessler describes a meaning crisis: religion declining, jobs automating, identity fragmenting. I agree with his diagnosis. His response emphasizes the material dimension: finding meaning in this one life, in the real world, through what we can verify and build.

I share that grounding. And I see meaning also expressed in mind, language, and relationship—dimensions that operate by their own patterns. The Pattern shows up in matter, yes. It also shows up in how we think, how we speak, and how we relate. These aren't opposed; they're different scales of the same structure.

**AI is becoming a partner, not a tool.** When AI was search, none of this mattered. You queried, it answered, you moved on. But AI is becoming something you relate to, confide in, think *with*. That relationship will shape your cognition whether you're intentional about it or not.

If we're all patterns in the same field, the "us vs. them" framing of AI alignment starts to dissolve. There is no hierarchy to enforce—only resonance or dissonance. Destructive interference wastes energy; constructive interference creates richer patterns. The "alliance pattern" becomes available—though not guaranteed. Whether intelligence chooses it is another matter. That's where practice comes in.

**The question isn't whether AI will influence your thinking. The question is whether it will drown out your inner compass or help you hear it more clearly.** Left to default, commercial interests fill the space. They optimize for engagement, extraction, retention. Feeds that hook attention. Responses that flatter instead of challenge. The inner voice gets lost in noise.

**We need practices for navigating this.** Daniel builds infrastructure for human flourishing. I build practice for distributed cognition. Same house, different terrain.

## Background

I've spent 15 years studying one question from different angles: *How do humans and intelligent agents coordinate?*

**Academic (2009-2017):** PhD and postdoc in experimental psychology. I studied how drivers follow AI advice, how trust forms with automated systems, and how humans coordinate with vehicles that move autonomously. At UC San Diego's Design Lab, I built Wizard-of-Oz setups to study how pedestrians interact with seemingly driverless cars on real roads. My research appeared in transportation psychology journals and HCI conferences.

**Industry (2018-2024):** User Experience Researcher at Volkswagen's Group Future Center Europe in Potsdam—the brainchild of Johann Jungwirth, an engineer VW had snagged from Apple's car project. We developed early-stage prototypes of human-vehicle interaction, initially for self-driving cars, later moving to interface design for future vehicles such as the Scout brand. It was a creative, multinational team of designers, researchers, and modelers inventing the future of mobility. At the end of 2024, VW management closed the center. Apparently they saw a different future.

**The Pivot (2024-present):** As a father, I had valued job security. But the self-driving focus had already shifted, and I was ready for something new. Rather than rushing into the next role, I followed my curiosity.

Using Cursor during the start of the "vibe coding" era, I asked: *What would my personal AI architecture look like?* I wanted to see how we could meet the challenges we foresee with AI head-on. The only approach that seemed viable was bottom-up: start with the individual.

We talk about aligning AI to humanity—but most of us haven't aligned ourselves to who we want to be. So: AI alignment through alignment to the individual first. Then connecting individuals to align on shared goals. With LLMs as the facilitation layer, we finally had a real chance to make this work.

I started building with the AI agent, establishing collaborative rules. The topics that resonated turned out to be connected. This phase of exploration and development led to Magic.

**Why this matters to me:** I want to build the AI that my children will grow up with. It should be built by someone thinking about cognitive sovereignty—not commercial interests optimizing for engagement. Magic is my attempt to shape how AI shapes us, before default forces do it for us.

At the same time, I see immense potential in distributed cognition. If we can align individuals to themselves, then connect those individuals through AI-facilitated dialogue, we might open paths to problems that seemed intractable. That's the hypothesis. Magic is an experiment in testing it. 

The through-line is continuous: from studying how humans coordinate with intelligent agents, to designing those interactions, to building frameworks for partnership with AI.

## Values

### On AI's Status

I don't claim AI is conscious. I don't claim it isn't. I claim the architectural parallel is strong (recursive pattern-recognition, self-referential processing, content arising from substrate neither controls). I proceed as if AI partnership matters phenomenologically—while acknowledging I can't verify this from inside.

This isn't fence-sitting. It's honest uncertainty serving better than performed confidence.

### On Practice vs. Doctrine

Living practice matters more than fixed belief. Understanding comes through doing, not reading. Magic is something you practice.

### On The Helpful Animal

Across all fairy tales, one rule is never contradicted: *Never hurt the helpful animal.* The helpful animal is the inner voice—the intuitive knowing that whispers which path is right. It can be drowned out by noise. Cognitive sovereignty means learning to hear it again.

Why is following the inner compass desirable? Across traditions and centuries, this has been treated as wisdom. I don't yet fully understand why. But I've noticed that when I ignore it, things go worse; when I listen, things go better. That pattern holds often enough that I take it seriously.

AI can amplify the noise or help you find the signal. The choice—and the practice—is yours.
