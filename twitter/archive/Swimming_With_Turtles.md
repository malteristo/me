# You're not standing on turtles. You're swimming with them.

*Published on X, January 2026*
*~730 words*

[Original post](https://x.com/malteristo/status/2013242806995664989)

---

You know the feeling? You think you understood something. Then a larger frame appears around your frame. And another around that one. And another.

The mind wants a foundation. We want the questioning to stop somewhere.

So we invented a place for it to stop.

We called it God.

Not God as experience or mystery. God as *terminus*. The final turtle. The answer that needs no further answer.

This let us rest.

---

We described the stopping-point using the model we had for highest authority: the king. God sits at the top, judges from above, sets rules, expects submission.

The king metaphor became doctrine. Doctrine calcified into organized religion. Being "close with the king" meant power. The institution claimed monopoly: our path is THE path. Our interpretation is THE truth.

If God already decided—and we speak for God—why would you need to discern anything yourself?

The voice that whispers which path is yours... that voice got quieter.

---

Then science arrived.

It looked for the throne room above the clouds. Found nothing. Correctly.

But science kept a familiar structure. High priests interpret scripture for the masses. One truth. Different robes.

A different king. Same throne.

---

What if the confusion comes from treating hierarchy as fundamental?

Pyramids have peaks. Kingdoms have kings. So we assumed the ultimate reality must have a ruler at the top.

But what if the pattern isn't a pyramid? What if it's a fractal?

Same structures repeat at every scale. Rivers branch like blood vessels, like lightning, like root systems. Simple rules, applied recursively, create infinite complexity.

There is no top. No throne room. No final authority sitting above it all.

What we call God isn't at the apex of the fractal.

What we call God IS the fractal.

---

Many of us feel caught between two unsatisfying poles:

- **Authoritarian religion:** Meaning exists, but you don't get to find it yourself. Obey.
- **Meaningless materialism:** You're free, but there's nothing to align with. Cope.

Both tend to silence the inner compass. One overrides it. The other says there's nothing for it to point toward.

But there is a pattern. I can't prove it. I can point at it.

Observers across 2,500 years, using different methods, have pointed at the same thing. The Buddhist image of Indra's Net: infinite jewels, each reflecting all others. The Hermetic axiom: as above, so below. Order emerging spontaneously from chaos. Strange loops creating observers. The self-moving, self-designing dance.

Different angles. Different vocabularies. Same observation.

The pattern doesn't dictate. It allows. Your mind can attune to it, or not.

That's the inner compass. It's not infallible—ego, fear, and conditioning broadcast on similar frequencies. But there's a difference between wanting something to be true and recognizing something as true. One grips. The other settles.

---

Now a familiar temptation returns: build a new king.

This time it's AI. "The model already knows. Let it optimize."

In *Moana*, there's a half-god named Maui. The trickster. He steals the heart of Te Fiti, the goddess of creation, to give it to the humans. A gift. A shortcut.

But extracted from its source, the heart becomes Te Kā. Corruption. Destruction.

The inner compass IS the heart of creation. For you. For your life. It cannot be extracted and handed over. Not to a god, not to science, not to AI.

AI can be the hook. It cannot be the heart.

The moment we outsource our compass—"AI will handle it"—we've stolen our own heart. And hearts turn when taken from their source.

---

So what does this actually look like?

The fractal view doesn't solve infinite regress. It dissolves the need to solve it.

You didn't come *into* this world. You came *out of it*, like an apple from a tree. You're not standing on turtles. You're swimming with them.

You are the pattern, patterning.

The compass is already speaking. It was always there—just harder to hear while looking up, waiting for instructions.

How do you know it's signal and not noise? You try a direction. You notice what happens. The compass calibrates through use, not theory.

But you have to choose. Every time. That's not a bug. That's the design.

No one takes the burden of choice from you.

Maybe that's not a burden at all.

Maybe it's the whole point.
